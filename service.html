What is a Caste Certificate?

A Caste Certificate is proof of one's belonging to a particular caste as specified in the Indian Constitution. The Government felt that the Scheduled Castes, Tribes and some economically backward castes need special encouragement and opportunities to progress at the same pace as the rest of the people in the country. As a part of the Protective Discrimination system in India, certain special privileges have been granted to this group of citizens. Such privileges include reservation of posts in Government service and seats in Legislatures, waiving of admission fees (in whole or part) in colleges and schools, quotas in jobs, relaxations in upper age limits while applying for certain jobs and so on.

Any individual regardless of age or gender can obtain a Caste Certificate from Maharashtra Government if he/she fulfills the requirements mentioned below.

Eligibility

Any individual regardless of age or gender can obtain a Caste Certificate from Maharashtra Government if he/she fulfills the following conditions:

1. Should be an Indian citizen
The individual must have Indian citizenship.

2. Should belong to any one of the reserved categories
The individual must belong to any one of the following caste category:

  • SC - Scheduled Caste
  • ST - Scheduled Tribe
  • VJ- Vimukta Jati
  • NT - Nomadic Tribe
  • OBC - Other Backward Class
  • SBC - Special Backward Class
  • SEBC - Special Economical Backward Class

Note: For now, we provide caste certificates only from NAGPUR
Hence to avail the service, the caste certificate of the father or grandfather must also be from Nagpur District.

Driving License

Proof of Permanent Address (Any 1)

Voter ID Card

Property Tax Receipt

Note:
The address should be correct and updated in the document which you would send.

Additional Documents

Passport Size photo of applicant

Aadhar card of beneficiary and applicant

School Leaving Certificate of Benificiary and his/her father (and of grandfather if available)

Ration Card

Caste Certificate of father (and of grandfather if available)

Copy of Kotwal Book

Note: If the applicant and the beneficiary are the same person, then the documents need not be sent twice.
Important: Atleast one of the document provided for the caste verification must be from or before the year mentioned below

  • SC - Scheduled Caste : 1950
  • ST - Scheduled Tribe : 1950
  • VJ- Vimukta Jati : 1960
  • NT - Nomadic Tribe : 1960
  • SEBC - Special Economical Backward Class : 1961
  • OBC - Other Backward Class : 1967
  • SBC - Special Backward Class : 1967

[IMP] Non-creamy Layer Certificate

For categories except SC and ST, a non-creamy layer (NCL) certificate is also needs to be attached along with the other documents. It proves that the family income of the beneficiary is below Rs. 8 lakh taken as an average over the last 3 years.
Although, individuals can also obtain a plain caste certificate without NCL, but would not be eligible for several benefits provided by the government to their caste.

The following document is needed for the same

Income Certificate (3 years)
